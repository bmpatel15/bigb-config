# Protected satsang terminology

These terms stay in their original form. Explain one naturally in the surrounding prose the
first time it carries weight in the note — then just use it. Never replace a term with an
imprecise English stand-in, and never bracket it with a translation on every appearance.

The fuller reference, if a term is unfamiliar or a spelling is uncertain, is the sibling skill's
glossary: `~/.claude/skills/gujarati-pdf-translator/references/glossary.md` (with
`names-and-titles.md` alongside it for canonical spellings of people and places).

## Core terms

| Term | What it points at | Do not render as |
|---|---|---|
| Agna | The commands/instructions of God and Guru, as a lived discipline | "orders", "rules" |
| Upasana | Understanding of and devotional relationship to the form of God | "worship" |
| Nischay | Firm conviction in the manifest form of God | "belief", "faith" |
| Nishtha | Steady, settled conviction; unwavering allegiance | "loyalty" |
| Paksh | Taking the side of God, Guru, and Satsang | "bias", "partisanship" |
| Satsang | The fellowship; also the practice of association with truth | "the community" alone |
| Seva | Selfless service offered as devotion | "volunteering" |
| Sant | A true, God-realized sadhu | "saint", "monk" |
| Satpurush | The God-realized Sant through whom God is attained | "holy man" |
| Guru | The living spiritual master in the Gunatit lineage | "teacher", "mentor" |
| Maharaj | Bhagwan Swaminarayan | — |
| Swami | Aksharbrahman Gunatitanand Swami (and, in address, the Guru) | — |
| Aksharbrahman | The eternal, conscious abode and ideal devotee | "the absolute" |
| Parabrahman | Purushottam, the supreme God, distinct from and greater than Aksharbrahman | "the divine" |
| Ekantik Dharma | The fourfold perfection of dharma, gnan, vairagya, bhakti | "true religion" |
| Gunatit | Transcending the three gunas | "enlightened" |
| Prapti | What one has received — the attainment of God and Guru in this life | "blessing", "privilege" |
| Divyabhav | Seeing the divine in God, Guru, and devotees | "positive thinking" |
| Manushyabhav | Perceiving human fault or ordinariness where the divine is | "criticism" |

## Also common in this vault

`vrutti` (the direction of mental attention) · `mahima` (glory, greatness) · `murti` (the sacred
form) · `mansi puja` (mental worship) · `katha` · `sabha` · `dhyan` · `samadhi` · `nirvikalp` ·
`sadhana` · `swabhav` (ingrained temperament, disposition) · `prasang` (an account or incident) ·
`granth` (scripture) · `ahnik` (the daily round of practices) · `shlok` · `kirtan` · `darshan` ·
`vairagya` · `gnan` · `bhakti` · `dharma`.

## Honorifics

Preserve them; never drop one to shorten a sentence: Bhagwan, Maharaj, Swami, Swamishri,
Guruhari, Param Pujya, Brahmaswarup, Sant, Bapa.

## Naming the figures

Use full names on first mention: Bhagwan Swaminarayan, Aksharbrahman Gunatitanand Swami,
Bhagatji Maharaj, Shastriji Maharaj, Yogiji Maharaj, Pramukh Swami Maharaj, Mahant Swami Maharaj.

In this vault "Maharaj and Swami" together means Bhagwan Swaminarayan and Gunatitanand Swami.

## The hard rule

`10 - Context/satsang.md` states it directly: **never fabricate quotes from the Vachanamrut or
Swamini Vato.** See `writing-style.md` for how this extends to prasangs and attributed actions.
