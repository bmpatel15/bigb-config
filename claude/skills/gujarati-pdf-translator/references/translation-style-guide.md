# Translation Style Guide

## Target register

The English should be:

- **clear** — a first-time reader follows every sentence;
- **dignified** — worthy of the subject matter;
- **devotional where the source is devotional** — reverence carried in tone, not
  gushing adjectives;
- **faithful** — nothing added, nothing dropped, nothing "improved";
- **elegant** — polished, well-paced prose;
- **warm but not sentimental** — feeling conveyed by what happens, not by inserted
  emotion words;
- **formal enough for publication**, natural enough for modern readers.

The test: it should read like a carefully edited BAPS English publication — not like
machine translation, and not like a lifestyle blog.

## Overly literal syntax → natural English

Gujarati word order, participle chains, and idioms must not be copied into English.

> ❌ "Having done darshan of Maharaj, joy in the heart of the devotee was not being
> contained."
>
> ✅ "After Maharaj's darshan, the devotee's heart overflowed with joy."

> ❌ "By Swamishri, to all the youths, agna was given that satsang do daily."
>
> ✅ "Swamishri gave the youths the agna to do satsang daily."

> ❌ "In the village, of the sadhus, much insult happened."
>
> ✅ "In the village, the sadhus were badly insulted."

## Over-translated terms → preferred transliteration

> ❌ "He obeyed his guru's order and kept faith in the idol."
>
> ✅ "He lived by his guru's agna and kept nishtha in the murti."

> ❌ "Through worship and good company, his loyalty deepened."
>
> ✅ "Through upasana and satsang, his nishtha deepened."

> ❌ "He went to the temple for a viewing of the statue."
>
> ✅ "He went to the mandir for darshan of the murti."

## Honorifics: poor → improved

> ❌ "Swami told the devotees…" (source: "Brahmaswarup Yogiji Maharaj told…")
>
> ✅ "Brahmaswarup Yogiji Maharaj told the devotees…" — never trim a title the source
> gives in full.

> ❌ "Pramukh, the leader of the organization, said…"
>
> ✅ "Pramukh Swami Maharaj said…" — a title used as a name is a name.

## Pronouns: poor → improved

Gujarati respectful plurals (તેઓ, એમણે) hide number and identity that English needs.

> ❌ "He told him that he should serve him with love." (three referents, all "he")
>
> ✅ "Yogiji Maharaj told the young sadhu to serve Shastriji Maharaj with love."

Repeat the name or title rather than tolerate ambiguity — in satsang prose, repeating
"Swamishri" is natural, not clumsy.

## Faithful vs. embellished

Do not intensify, modernize, or decorate.

> Source (plain): "સ્વામીશ્રી રાજી થયા." — "Swamishri was pleased."
>
> ❌ "Swamishri's face lit up with boundless, radiant joy."
>
> ✅ "Swamishri was pleased."

> ❌ "This life-changing moment transformed his spiritual journey forever."
> (source says: "તે દિવસથી તેમને સત્સંગનો રંગ લાગ્યો.")
>
> ✅ "From that day, the color of satsang took hold of him." — or, more naturally:
> "From that day, he was devoted to satsang." Choose natural English, but never add
> drama the Gujarati does not contain.

## What to avoid absolutely

- marketing language ("world-class", "iconic", "transformative");
- casual internet tone ("amazing", "super humble");
- generic inspirational filler ("journey", "empowering", "unlock");
- modernized theology (recasting doctrine in psychological or secular vocabulary);
- invented emotional language (tears, trembling, radiance not present in the source);
- summarizing repetitive or list-like passages ("and many other devotees" for a list of
  names — translate every name).

## Rhythm and flow

- Break very long Gujarati sentences at natural clause boundaries; never split a
  quotation from its speaker attribution.
- Combine only fragments that Gujarati typography split arbitrarily; never merge two
  distinct sentences of content.
- Preserve paragraph boundaries exactly; preserve heading levels.
- Keep rhetorical questions as questions; keep exclamations as exclamations, without
  multiplying exclamation marks.
