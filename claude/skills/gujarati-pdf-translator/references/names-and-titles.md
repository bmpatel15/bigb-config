# Names, Titles, and Canonical Spellings

**Rules (absolute):**

1. **Never translate a proper name.** People, gurus, sadhus, devotees, villages,
   cities, mandirs, organizations, festivals, scriptures, publications, historical
   locations, and titles used as names are transliterated or rendered with their
   established English spelling — never by the dictionary meaning of the words inside
   them. (A devotee named "Motibhai" is Motibhai, not "Pearl-brother.")
2. **Never shorten a title** unless the source or the user does.
3. **Never invent a spelling** for an uncertain reading — write `[Name uncertain]` and
   give the probable reading: `[Name uncertain: probably Haribhai]`.
4. **Use one spelling per name per document.** The first established spelling goes into
   the terminology log and is reused everywhere.
5. When the source alternates between a name and a title for the same person (e.g.
   "Maharaj" for Bhagwan Swaminarayan, "Swami" for Gunatitanand Swami in Swamini Vato),
   keep the alternation — do not homogenize — but ensure the referent stays clear in
   English (Stage 7 of the workflow).

## Honorifics and respectful titles

Preserve these wherever they carry meaning or relationship; do not delete them to
shorten the English.

| Honorific | Usage note |
|---|---|
| Bhagwan | "God"; retained as part of names (Bhagwan Swaminarayan). |
| Maharaj | Standalone, usually Bhagwan Swaminarayan in satsang texts — confirm from context. |
| Swami | Title for sadhus; standalone in Swamini Vato context = Gunatitanand Swami. |
| Swamishri | Respectful reference to the present guru in BAPS publications (which guru depends on the era of the text). |
| Guruhari | Title of the present guru. |
| Param Pujya | "Most revered"; keep as an untranslated title before a name. |
| Brahmaswarup | Title of gurus as the swarup of Aksharbrahman; never translate. |
| Pragat Brahmaswarup | Title of the present manifest guru; never translate. |
| Sant / Satpurush | The God-realized sadhu; keep as sant / Satpurush. |
| Sadhu | Renunciant; keep as sadhu (plural: sadhus). |
| Bhakta / Bhagat | Devotee; part of names (Pragji Bhakta, Jhina Bhagat) — never translate there. |
| Bapa | Affectionate address for the guru (Yogi Bapa; Pramukh Swami Maharaj as "Bapa"). |
| Guru | Keep as guru. |
| Acharya | Keep as acharya (also a formal office in the Swaminarayan Sampraday). |
| -ji suffix | Respectful suffix (Shastriji, Gopinathji, Thakorji); always keep. |
| -bhai / -ben | Respectful suffixes on lay names (Motibhai, Laduben); always keep. |

Never replace Swaminarayan/BAPS terminology with vocabulary of unrelated traditions
(e.g. do not render sadhu as "monk" when the source's specific term matters, mandir as
"temple" is acceptable general English but keep "mandir" in BAPS institutional names).
