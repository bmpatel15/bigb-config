# Glossary of Protected Satsang Terminology

**Core principle:** when no single English word captures the complete meaning of a
Gujarati term, **transliteration is preferred**. Translating such a term with a
one-word English "equivalent" weakens, narrows, distorts, or secularizes it. Keep the
transliterated term, gloss it briefly on first use only when useful, and use it
consistently thereafter.

**How to use this glossary**

- *Preferred treatment* tells you the default. *Context-dependent* lists cases where a
  different rendering is legitimate (record such choices in the terminology log).
- *Prohibited* lists simplistic renderings that must not replace the term in satsang
  contexts.
- Capitalization: lowercase for general terms; capitalize metaphysical entities and
  divine names (Akshar, Aksharbrahman, Parabrahman, Purushottam) — see
  `transliteration-guide.md`.
- Plurals take a plain "s" (murtis, kirtans); possessives a normal apostrophe.
- Do not italicize recurring terms; prefer clean prose.

**Adding a term:** copy any entry below as a template, keep the same fields, and add it
in alphabetical order. Record the decision in the document's terminology log too.

## agna (આજ્ઞા)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **agna**.
- **Meaning range:** the loving command or spiritual directive of Bhagwan or the guru,
  carrying divine authority and willingly embraced by the devotee; also the discipline
  of living by such directives ("agna palan" — observing agna).
- **Context-dependent:** in purely secular narrative (a king instructing a minister),
  "command" or "instruction" is acceptable. In any satsang context, keep agna.
- **Prohibited:** "order", "orders", bare "command" for the devotional concept.
- **Example:** "Swamishri gave the agna to build a mandir in the village." / "He lived
  his entire life within agna."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## Akshar (અક્ષર)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **Akshar**.
- **Meaning range:** the eternal, imperishable divine reality — Aksharbrahman; by
  extension the divine abode (Akshardham) and, in BAPS upasana, the ideal devotee and
  gunatit guru in whom Akshar is manifest.
- **Context-dependent:** in the compound "Akshar-Purushottam" (the darshan, the
  upasana, mandir names), always keep as is.
- **Prohibited:** "the imperishable", "letter" (a literal dictionary sense that is
  almost never the meaning in satsang texts).
- **Example:** "One becomes aksharrup and offers bhakti to Purushottam."
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized as a metaphysical entity; lowercase only in
  derived adjectives the source itself treats generically (aksharrup).

## Aksharbrahman (અક્ષરબ્રહ્મ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **Aksharbrahman** (one word).
- **Meaning range:** the second-highest metaphysical reality in the Akshar-Purushottam
  darshan — eternal, beyond maya, forever in the service of Parabrahman; manifest as
  the divine abode Akshardham and as the gunatit guru.
- **Context-dependent:** titles such as "Brahmaswarup" applied to gurus derive from
  this concept; keep both as given in the source.
- **Prohibited:** "the imperishable Brahman" as a replacement (acceptable only as a
  first-use gloss), "God" (Aksharbrahman is distinct from Parabrahman — conflating them
  is a doctrinal error).
- **Example:** "Gunatitanand Swami is revered as the manifestation of Aksharbrahman."
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized; never "aksharbrahman".

## atmabuddhi (આત્મબુદ્ધિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **atmabuddhi**.
- **Meaning range:** profound inner identification and oneness — regarding God and the
  guru as one's own atma, dearer than one's own self; intimate, inseparable attachment.
- **Context-dependent:** first-use gloss such as "atmabuddhi — intimate oneness with
  the guru" is helpful.
- **Prohibited:** "self-intelligence", "self-mindedness", any literal decomposition.
- **Example:** "He served with atmabuddhi, never regarding himself as separate from his
  guru."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## bhakti (ભક્તિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **bhakti**; "devotion" is acceptable in plain
  narrative where no doctrinal weight is carried.
- **Meaning range:** loving devotion offered to God; the fourth limb of ekantik dharma
  (with dharma, gnan, vairagya); specific practices of devotion.
- **Context-dependent:** in technical or doctrinal lists (dharma, gnan, vairagya,
  bhakti) always keep bhakti.
- **Prohibited:** "worship" (that is upasana/puja territory), "faith".
- **Example:** "Bhakti coupled with the knowledge of God's greatness becomes
  unshakable."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## chintavan (ચિંતવન)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **chintavan**.
- **Meaning range:** sustained contemplative dwelling of the mind on the murti or
  virtues of God and the guru; devotional visualization and recollection.
- **Prohibited:** "thinking", "worrying" (a false cognate of ચિંતા).
- **Example:** "While traveling he remained absorbed in chintavan of Maharaj's murti."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## darshan (દર્શન)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **darshan**.
- **Meaning range:** the reverent, blessed act of beholding God, the guru, or the
  murti — and the grace received through it; "doing darshan" is standard satsang
  English.
- **Context-dependent:** in academic/philosophical contexts, દર્શન can mean a school of
  philosophy ("the Akshar-Purushottam darshan") — keep darshan there too.
- **Prohibited:** "seeing", "viewing", "a look".
- **Example:** "Thousands waited for hours for Swamishri's darshan."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase (capitalize only in proper names such as "the
  Akshar-Purushottam Darshan" when the source treats it as a title).

## dasbhav (દાસભાવ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **dasbhav**.
- **Meaning range:** the devotional sentiment of being God's das — loving, willing
  humility and servitude before God, guru, and devotees; the opposite of ego.
- **Prohibited:** "slavery", "servility", "slave mentality".
- **Example:** "Despite being the head of the Sanstha, he served everyone with
  dasbhav."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## divyabhav (દિવ્યભાવ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **divyabhav**.
- **Meaning range:** perceiving divinity — seeing God, the guru, devotees, and their
  actions as divine, free of worldly fault-finding; the counterpart of manushyabhav.
- **Prohibited:** "divine feeling" as a flat substitute; "positivity".
- **Example:** "Keep divyabhav toward every satsangi; see the divine at work in them."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## ekantik dharma (એકાંતિક ધર્મ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **ekantik dharma** (two words).
- **Meaning range:** the integrated spiritual path uniting dharma, gnan, vairagya, and
  bhakti; the state of the ekantik bhakta whose devotion is single-pointed and complete.
- **Prohibited:** "exclusive religion", "one-pointed duty", any literal decomposition.
- **Example:** "The Satpurush establishes ekantik dharma in the lives of devotees."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase (capitalize only at sentence start or in titles).

## gunatit (ગુણાતીત)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **gunatit**.
- **Meaning range:** transcending the three gunas of maya (sattva, rajas, tamas);
  epithet of Aksharbrahman and of the guru parampara ("the Gunatit gurus").
- **Prohibited:** "beyond qualities" as a replacement (fine as a first-use gloss only).
- **Example:** "A gunatit sadhu remains unaffected by praise or insult."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase as an adjective; capitalized in names and titles
  (Gunatitanand Swami, the Gunatit guru parampara).

## khap (ખપ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **khap**.
- **Meaning range:** intense inner need and yearning for God, liberation, and spiritual
  attainment; earnest spiritual want that drives effort.
- **Prohibited:** "need", "requirement", "use".
- **Example:** "Without khap, even daily satsang remains superficial."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## mahima (મહિમા)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **mahima**.
- **Meaning range:** the true spiritual glory and greatness of God, the guru, the
  sadhus, devotees, and satsang — and, crucially, the devotee's *realized
  understanding* of that greatness ("samajvo mahima" — to understand mahima).
- **Context-dependent:** where the source uses it loosely of a place or event,
  "glory"/"greatness" may fit; in doctrinal use, keep mahima.
- **Prohibited:** bare "greatness" or "glory" for the devotional concept of understood
  greatness.
- **Example:** "As his understanding of Swamishri's mahima deepened, his seva became
  effortless."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## manan (મનન)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **manan**.
- **Meaning range:** deliberate reflection on spiritual teachings one has heard —
  second of the triad shravan (listening), manan (reflection), nididhyas
  (assimilation).
- **Prohibited:** "thinking it over" as a flat substitute in doctrinal contexts.
- **Example:** "After katha he would sit alone in manan on what Swami had said."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## manushyabhav (મનુષ્યભાવ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **manushyabhav**.
- **Meaning range:** perceiving mere humanness in God or the guru — attributing human
  limitations, motives, or faults to them; the obstacle that divyabhav removes.
- **Prohibited:** "human feelings" as a flat substitute; "humanity".
- **Example:** "Doubts arise when a devotee views the Satpurush with manushyabhav."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## murti (મૂર્તિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **murti**.
- **Meaning range:** the sacred image of God, ritually installed and venerated as a
  living form of the divine; by extension, the cherished form of God or guru held in
  the heart ("Swamishri's murti remained before his eyes").
- **Context-dependent:** none that justify "idol". In neutral art-historical passages
  "image" may appear alongside murti, but murti stays the primary term.
- **Prohibited:** **"idol"** (never — it imports a hostile theology), "statue",
  "icon".
- **Example:** "Maharaj installed the murti of Gopinathji in Gadhada."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## nididhyas (નિદિધ્યાસ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **nididhyas**.
- **Meaning range:** deep, repeated contemplation and assimilation of a spiritual truth
  until it becomes one's own lived conviction — third of shravan, manan, nididhyas.
- **Prohibited:** "meditation" as a flat substitute (that suggests dhyan), "profound
  thought".
- **Example:** "Through constant nididhyas, the words of the Vachanamrut became his
  very nature."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## nishchay (નિશ્ચય)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **nishchay**.
- **Meaning range:** firm, settled conviction in God — specifically, unshakable
  conviction in the supremacy and divinity of the manifest (pragat) form of God and the
  guru; the bedrock of a devotee's spiritual life.
- **Prohibited:** "certainty", "determination", "decision".
- **Example:** "Once his nishchay in Maharaj was complete, no hardship could move him."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## nishtha (નિષ્ઠા)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **nishtha**.
- **Meaning range:** deep, resolute spiritual fidelity and grounded conviction — firm,
  exclusive devotion toward one's God, guru, and dharma that endures testing.
- **Prohibited:** "loyalty", "faith", "commitment" as one-word replacements.
- **Example:** "He maintained unwavering nishtha—deep, resolute spiritual fidelity—
  toward his guru." (Later simply: "His nishtha remained firm.")
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## paksh (પક્ષ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **paksh**.
- **Meaning range:** steadfast allegiance — consciously taking and keeping the side of
  God, the guru, and satsang, especially when they are criticized or tested; "rakhvo
  paksh" — to keep paksh.
- **Context-dependent:** in secular prose (a legal or political "side"), "side" is
  fine. In satsang usage, keep paksh.
- **Prohibited:** "side", "faction", "partisanship" for the devotional concept.
- **Example:** "Even when the village turned against the sadhus, he kept the paksh of
  satsang."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## Parabrahman (પરબ્રહ્મ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **Parabrahman**.
- **Meaning range:** the supreme God — Purushottam Narayan, highest of all metaphysical
  realities, transcending even Aksharbrahman; in Swaminarayan upasana, Bhagwan
  Swaminarayan.
- **Prohibited:** "the supreme Brahman" as a replacement (gloss only), generic "God"
  where the source is making the precise doctrinal distinction.
- **Example:** "Aksharbrahman eternally serves Parabrahman."
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized; never "parabrahman".

## pativratapanu (પતિવ્રતાપણું)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **pativratapanu**.
- **Meaning range:** exclusive, undivided fidelity — the single-hearted faithfulness of
  a pativrata applied spiritually: devotion to one God and one guru alone, admitting no
  rival attachment.
- **Context-dependent:** when the source speaks literally of a wife's vow, translate
  the sentence naturally but keep the term where it functions as a concept.
- **Prohibited:** literal marital wording ("wifely devotion", "chastity") when the use
  is spiritual or metaphorical.
- **Example:** "He kept pativratapanu toward his guru — his devotion admitted no
  divided allegiance."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## pragat (પ્રગટ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **pragat**.
- **Meaning range:** manifest, visibly present here and now — God or the Aksharbrahman
  guru present before one's eyes; "pragat Bhagwan", "pragat guru Hari".
- **Prohibited:** "current", "alive", "existing" — these strip the doctrinal weight of
  divine manifestation.
- **Example:** "Liberation is attained through the pragat form of God."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase as an adjective; capitalized inside titles the source
  capitalizes (Pragat Brahmaswarup Mahant Swami Maharaj).

## prapti (પ્રાપ્તિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **prapti**.
- **Meaning range:** spiritual attainment — what one has received through the grace of
  God and the guru: their association, the Satsang, the path itself; "What prapti we
  have!" expresses gratitude for that attainment.
- **Prohibited:** "acquisition", "gain", "achievement".
- **Example:** "Yogiji Maharaj would often say: realize the greatness of your prapti."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## priti (પ્રીતિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **priti**.
- **Meaning range:** deep, tender, loving affection for God and the guru — devotional
  love that binds the heart; "priti karvi" — to cultivate such love.
- **Prohibited:** "love" as a flat substitute in doctrinal passages (acceptable in
  plain narrative), "fondness".
- **Example:** "His priti for Shriji Maharaj showed in his eyes whenever he spoke of
  him."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## Purushottam (પુરુષોત્તમ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **Purushottam**.
- **Meaning range:** the supreme Person — Parabrahman; in Swaminarayan upasana,
  Bhagwan Swaminarayan as the highest reality ("Purushottam Narayan").
- **Prohibited:** "the supreme being" as a replacement (gloss only), "best of men".
- **Example:** "The mandir enshrines the upasana of Akshar and Purushottam."
- **Capitalization:** always capitalized; never "purushottam".

## ruchi (રુચિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **ruchi**.
- **Meaning range:** inner taste or inclination — what the mind naturally relishes;
  one's spiritual disposition ("his ruchi was for katha and kirtan"); also the guru's
  preference which devotees seek to serve ("according to Swamishri's ruchi").
- **Prohibited:** "interest", "hobby", "liking" as flat substitutes in devotional use.
- **Example:** "He shaped his seva according to the ruchi of his guru."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## samagam (સમાગમ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **samagam**.
- **Meaning range:** close spiritual association — being in the company of the guru or
  sadhus and imbibing their words and virtues; "karvo samagam" — to seek such company.
- **Prohibited:** "meeting", "gathering", "contact".
- **Example:** "He traveled every month to Sarangpur for Swami's samagam."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## satsang (સત્સંગ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **satsang**.
- **Meaning range:** association with the sat — with God, the Satpurush, the shastras,
  and the company of devotees; the practice of such association (attending sabha,
  katha); and the fellowship/community itself ("he was brought into satsang in 1962").
- **Context-dependent:** when it names the community as an institution the source may
  capitalize ("the Satsang"); follow the source.
- **Prohibited:** "good company", "religious gathering", "congregation" as
  replacements.
- **Example:** "Through the satsang of Gunatitanand Swami, the glory of Maharaj was
  understood."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase by default; "Satsang" when used as a proper name for
  the fellowship.

## seva (સેવા)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **seva**.
- **Meaning range:** devotional service — service of God, guru, mandir, satsang, or
  fellow beings offered as worship, without expectation; both the act and the attitude.
- **Prohibited:** "work", "volunteering", "duty", "service" alone where the devotional
  register matters (plain "service" is acceptable in neutral narrative).
- **Example:** "She regarded cooking for the sadhus not as labor but as seva."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## suhradbhav (સુહૃદભાવ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **suhradbhav**.
- **Meaning range:** heartfelt goodwill and friendship among devotees — mutual
  affection, harmony, and the absence of ill-will; a hallmark teaching of Yogiji
  Maharaj.
- **Prohibited:** "friendliness", "good feelings", "brotherhood" as flat substitutes.
- **Example:** "Yogiji Maharaj prayed daily that suhradbhav flourish among all
  satsangis."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## swarup (સ્વરૂપ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **swarup**; do **not** automatically render as
  "form".
- **Meaning range:** essential form, nature, true being — God's svarup (his essential
  divine nature), the guru as the swarup of Akshar, "swarupnishtha" (conviction in the
  true nature). Context decides whether the emphasis is visible form or essential
  nature.
- **Context-dependent:** in titles (Brahmaswarup, Pragat Brahmaswarup) it is part of a
  proper title — never translate. In philosophical passages a gloss ("swarup — his
  essential nature") can help on first use.
- **Prohibited:** automatic "form"; "shape"; "image".
- **Example:** "To understand the swarup of the Satpurush is the essence of gnan."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase standalone; capitalized inside titles (Brahmaswarup).

## upasana (ઉપાસના)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **upasana**.
- **Meaning range:** the philosophical understanding of God's nature *and* the mode of
  devotional worship that flows from it — a doctrine-laden term ("Akshar-Purushottam
  upasana"), not a generic act of worship.
- **Prohibited:** "worship" as a one-word replacement.
- **Example:** "Shastriji Maharaj consecrated the murtis of Akshar and Purushottam,
  giving the upasana concrete form."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase; follow the source for titled uses ("the
  Akshar-Purushottam Upasana").

## vrutti (વૃત્તિ)

- **Preferred treatment:** keep as **vrutti**.
- **Meaning range:** the flow or inclination of the mind — the stream of attention and
  thought; "keeping one's vrutti on the murti"; the tendencies that pull the mind
  outward or inward.
- **Prohibited:** "attitude", "occupation", "livelihood" (a false cognate of વૃત્તિ in
  secular Gujarati — check context), "tendency" as a flat substitute in doctrinal use.
- **Example:** "During puja he drew his vrutti back again and again to Maharaj's
  murti."
- **Capitalization:** lowercase.

## Quick reference: prohibited one-word substitutions

| Term | Never render as |
|---|---|
| agna | order |
| paksh | side |
| nishchay | certainty |
| nishtha | loyalty, faith |
| upasana | worship |
| mahima | greatness |
| satsang | good company |
| seva | work |
| bhakti | faith |
| darshan | seeing |
| murti | **idol**, statue |
| swarup | form (automatic) |
| samagam | meeting |
| dasbhav | slavery, servility |
| khap | need |
| prapti | acquisition |
| pativratapanu | wifely devotion (spiritual uses) |
