# Transliteration Guide

**Policy:** use common, established BAPS and English-language spellings — readable
English for general satsang readers — **not** academic Indological transliteration.

**Never add scholarly diacritics** (ā, ī, ū, ṛ, ṣ, ṇ, ś, ṁ) unless the user explicitly
requests scholarly transliteration. Write:

```text
agna   nishchay   nishtha   upasana   mahima   satsang   Aksharbrahman   Purushottam
```

not *ājñā*, *niṣṭhā*, *upāsanā*, *Akṣarabrahman*, *Puruṣottama*.

## Vowels

- Long and short vowels are not distinguished with marks: both અ/આ → "a"
  (Maharaj, satsang, darshan), both ઇ/ઈ → "i" (priti, murti), both ઉ/ઊ → "u"
  (guru, ruchi).
- Use "aa", "ee", "oo" only where an established spelling already uses them
  (e.g. some proper names); do not invent them.
- એ → "e" (seva), ઐ → "ai" (Vairagya), ઓ → "o" (yoga), ઔ → "au".
- The inherent "a" is dropped where Gujarati pronunciation drops it — especially
  word-finally: **darshan** (not darshana), **Akshar** (not Akshara), **swarup**
  (not swarupa), **gnan** (not gnana/jnana).

## Consonants

- જ્ઞ → **gn** per established satsang usage: **agna**, **gnan** (not ajna/jnana).
- ક્ષ → **ksh**: Akshar, moksha, Lakshmi.
- ચ → **ch**; છ → **chh**: chintavan; chhavani.
- શ and ષ → **sh**: darshan, nishtha, shastra.
- ળ → **l** (Gujarati retroflex l): Vartal, Dholera.
- વ → **v** in general vocabulary (seva, vrutti, bhav, vairagya) but **w** in
  established spellings: **Swami, Swaminarayan, swarup, Swamishri, ishwar** — follow
  the established-spellings table, not a mechanical rule.
- Aspirates are written with "h": bh (bhakti), dh (dharma), th (katha, nishtha),
  gh (Ghanshyam), chh (chhatri).
- Anusvara (ં) becomes the nasal that is actually pronounced: **sant**, **samp**,
  **Shantilal**, **panch**.

## Doubled consonants

Keep a doubled consonant only where the established spelling has one (vallabh,
suddha → shuddha) — do not double letters to mark Gujarati gemination unless the
customary spelling does (vrutti is customary; "vruti" is not).

## Names

- Use the established English spelling first (see `names-and-titles.md`); transliterate
  by these rules only when no established spelling exists.
- Keep the -ji, -bhai, -ben suffixes attached: Shastriji, Gopinathji, Motibhai,
  Laduben.
- Never anglicize or "correct" a name's spelling once established in the document.

## Sanskrit-derived terms

Prefer the satsang/Gujarati form over the Sanskrit academic form: **gnan** not jnana,
**agna** not ajna, **kirtan** not kīrtana, **mandir** not mandira, **darshan** not
darshana, **shastra** not śāstra.

## Capitalization

- **Always capitalized:** Akshar, Aksharbrahman, Parabrahman, Purushottam, Bhagwan,
  Akshardham, names and titles (Swamishri, Guruhari, Param Pujya, Brahmaswarup,
  Satpurush as a title).
- **Lowercase by default:** general terms — satsang, seva, bhakti, darshan, murti,
  agna, mahima, upasana, nishtha, nishchay, samagam, kirtan, katha, sabha, mandir,
  sadhu, guru (generic).
- Follow the source when a general term is used as a proper name ("the Satsang" as the
  fellowship; "the Akshar-Purushottam Upasana" as a titled doctrine).
- Sentence-start capitalization applies normally.

## Hyphenation

- Avoid hyphens inside single transliterated words (suhradbhav, atmabuddhi, dasbhav —
  one word each).
- Keep the customary hyphen in **Akshar-Purushottam** (upasana, darshan, maharaj
  contexts) when the source or established usage has it.
- Two-word terms stay two words: **ekantik dharma**.

## Apostrophes, plurals, possessives

- Plurals: plain "s", no apostrophe, no italics — murtis, sadhus, kirtans, prasangs,
  mandirs, satsangis.
- Possessives: normal English apostrophe — Swamishri's agna, Maharaj's murti.
- Never use an apostrophe to mark elided vowels (no "Swami'ni Vato") — write
  Swamini Vato.
